We noticed after running 5 different tests with different pattern lengths, unique
pages, and number of slots, MIN/Optimal was in the list of best algorithms every time.
This was expected, as it is the considered the baseline for judging an algorithms 
efficiecnt and reducing the amount of page faults. It appeared that FIFO was in the 
worst algorithm category almost every time, which was also expected, as it's the most primitive.
LRU and LFU were about tied in success based on our trials, probably due to the fact
that in the first couple of runs while filling the first few slots, they are almost identical.
The least recently used will most likely match the least frequently used until you have enough 
of the REF parsed through to determine which pages are most/least used. Overall, all trials 
performed as expected based on our understanding of the algorithms from class and proved as a 
helpful demonstration for our understanding.
